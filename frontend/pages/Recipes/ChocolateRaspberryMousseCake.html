Airy Chocolate Raspberry Mousse Cake

Ingredients:

Cake Layer:

Rasperry Mousse

Chocolate Mousse

Chocolate Ganache Glaze

Directions:

Cake Layer:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F.
  2. Grease a 9-inch springform pan.
  3. In a large bowl, mix the water, oil and vinegar until well combined.
  4. Add the flour, sugar, sifted cocoa powder, and baking soda. Mix until well combined.
  5. Bake at 350°F for 10 to 12 minutes until a toothpick inserted into the center comes our clean.
  6. Leave to cool completely making sure that you DO NOT remove it from its pan

Raspberry Mousse Layer:

  1. In a small bowl, sprinkle the gelatin over the 1 tbsp of water and let bloom for 5 minutes.
  2. Meanwhile, in a small saucepan, add the raspberries, lemon juice, vanilla extract, granulated sugar and water. Whisk constantly over medium-heat until the sauce coats the back of a spoon and ressembles thick jam-consistency.
  3. Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the bloomed gelatin mixture until well combined. Set the mixture aside to cool.
  4. In another bowl, whip the cream with an electric mixer until stiff peaks form. Mix in one quarter of the whipped cream to the cooled raspberry mixture. Then gently fold in the remaining cream.
  5. Spoon the raspberry mixture onto the cake layer.
  6. Place the cake into the fridge for at least 4 hours, or freeze for 2 hours.

Chocolate Mousse Layer:

  1. In a small bowl, sprinkle the gelatin over the 1 tbsp of water and let bloom for 5 minutes.
  2. Melt your chocolate using either a bowl placed on top of a pot of simmering water, or using the microwave.
  3. In a separate bowl placed on top of a pot of simmering water, or using the microwave, add in a 1/3 cup of the cream with the sugar. Heat until the sugar has dissolved. Remove from the heat and add the gelatin mixture until well combined.
  4. Add the cream mixture to the warmed chocolate and mix until well combined.
  5. In another bowl, whip the cream with an electric mixer until stiff peaks form. Mix in one quarter of the whipped cream to the cooled chocolate mixture. Then gently fold in the remaining whipped cream.
  6. Spoon the chocolate mousse onto the raspberry layer.
  7. Place the cake into the fridge for at least 4 hours, or freeze for 2 hours.

Chocolate Ganache Glaze:

  1. Prepare a tray with wax paper and place a wire rack over it. Take the cake out of the springform pan and place the cake on top of the wire rack. This setup should allow you to catch any ganache that is poured over. If frozen, leave the cake out for 1 hour to let it come up to room temperature.
  2. Place the chocolate into a bowl.
  3. In a small saucepan, or using the microwave, bring the cream to a boil. Add the cream to the bowl of chocolate in small increments until the chocolate is smooth. Then pour in the remaining cream. With a spatula, stir the ganache until smooth.
  4. Pour the chocolate ganache over the cake so that the entire cake is covered.
