Directions:

  1. Bloom the yeast to the warmed milk and set aside.
  2. In a mixer, cream the butter, sugar.
  3. Add the milk once the yeast has bloomed and your eggs. Mix until well-combined.
  4. Add the flour and salt. Mix until well-combined.
  5. Run the mixer for 10 minutes.
  6. Cover and set the dough aside in a well-oiled bowl until it has doubled in size.
  7. Meanwhile, make your black sesame paste by blending toasted black sesame seeds with 2 tablespoons of water. Add honey to taste.
  8. Once the dough has doubled in size, roll out the dough into a 9x11 inch rectangle.
  9. Cover spread the room-temperature butter onto the dough, leaving a 1/4 inch lip on the far side. Spread the sesame paste over the butter layer.
  10. Roll the dough starting from the close side and rolling out to the far side. Cut your rolled log of dough into 12 portions.
  11. Butter a 9x11 inch pan and place the rolls in the pan. Cover and place in a warm spot to let rise for 30-45 mins.
  12. Preheat the oven to 350°C. Bake rolls for 20-25 mins.
